History of the font

Roslindale is a variable serif font created and published by David Johnathan Ross, an incredible graphic design and font creator. Ross' inspiration stemmed from early Victorian types, specifically De Vinnee, a typeface made during the 1890s by the Central Type Foundry. As described by Ross, this type is “a balancing act of style, service, and sentiment.” Perfect for all of your print, posters, magazine, and website building needs, Roslindale is a perfect font of the month to ignite passion for your own designs, and as a student, commerical use is complete free. You can find this font as well as Ross' fanatastic other designs and works below.
